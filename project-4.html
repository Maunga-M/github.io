Power BI Dashboard Modelling

Data Analysis Power BI Business

Last Updated: 17 February 2026

Overview

This dashboard is built from a multi-year supply chain dataset covering commercial activity across several markets from January 2015 to January 2018. The data tracks each order in detail, including financial results and fulfillment performance. This makes it possible to analyze profitability together with operational execution and provides a complete view of overall business performance.

The dataset used in this project can be accessed on Mendeley Data: https://data.mendeley.com/datasets/8gx2fvg2k6/3

The dashboard is arranged to answer three escalating questions:

  • How is profitability moving?
  • Where is it concentrated?
  • Are business operations influencing the result?

Profitability Trends

To analyse trends, I grouped daily transactions into monthly totals to reduce short-term fluctuations and make longer-term patterns clearer. I created separate line charts for Revenue, Profit, and Units Sold so each metric could be viewed clearly without scale issues.

The measures (Total Profit, Total Revenue and Units Sold) were built to ensure consistent aggregation logic and reliable interaction with filters and date selections

Key Findings

Revenue, profit, and units sold move closely together for most of the period. This suggests that profit margins remained relatively stable and that changes in performance were largely driven by sales volume rather than pricing or cost shifts.

In late 2017, all three metrics fall sharply and stay at lower levels. Because revenue and profit continue to move in line with each other, the decline appears to be linked to reduced sales activity rather than a change in profitability per unit. The drop is sudden rather than gradual, which indicates a clear shift in performance during that period.

Geographic and Portfolio Structure

To examine geographic concentration, I used a field parameter that allow switching between Market, Region, and Country views. An additional parameter allows the metric to change between Revenue and Profit to make comparisons straightforward within the same visual.

Ranking was handled using RANKX together with a Top-N selector, to allow users to view either the Top 5 or Top 10 entities within any selected geographic scope. This reduced the need for multiple charts while keeping the analysis flexible and interactive.

Customer segment contribution was shown through a profit distribution pie chart, while product-level concentration was analysed using a ranked bar chart displaying the Top 5 categories by total profit.

Key Findings

At the country level, the United States generates the highest share of profit, showing clear concentration in a single market. However, when results are grouped by broader categories, Europe ranks as the top region and market overall. This means that while one country leads individually, Europe contributes more when viewed as a combined region.

The structure therefore shows concentration at the country level and broader distribution at the regional level. Performance depends heavily on the United States, but Europe provides collective strength across multiple countries. This balance reduces some risk, although results remain sensitive to changes in the leading country.

The Consumer segment accounts for more than half of total profit, with Corporate contributing a smaller share and Home Office the least. This shows that overall performance is largely driven by consumer demand, with limited balance across segments.

At the product level, Fishing generates the highest profit, followed by Cleats and Camping & Hiking. The gap between these leading categories and the rest suggests that profitability is concentrated in a small number of product lines rather than spread evenly across the portfolio.

Business Operations

To assess operational performance, I analysed Order Status as a percentage of total orders. This ensured results remain comparable across different filters and helps show how orders progress through the fulfillment process.

Shipping performance was evaluated using Total Orders, Average Profit per Order, and Average Shipping Days. This made it possible to compare delivery speed, profitability, and order volume across shipping modes within the same view.

Key Findings

Completed orders make up the largest share of total orders, while a noticeable portion remains in processing and pending stages. This suggests that revenue depends on how efficiently orders move through the system because delays can affect when sales are finalized and cash is received.

Shipping times follow the expected pattern, with Same Day being the fastest and Standard Class the slowest. However, profit per order does not increase significantly with faster delivery. Most orders are placed under Standard Class, which could mean that overall shipping profitability is driven more by volume than by premium delivery options.

Modelling Reference

The multi-year dataset was imported into Power BI Desktop and each column was reviewed for relevance to the analysis. Fields that were not required were removed to keep the model focused and efficient.

Column names were cleaned for clarity and inconsistencies were checked before loading the data into the model. The measures created for the dashboard are listed below.

Time Aggregation

MonthStart = DATE(YEAR(orders[Order Date]), MONTH(orders[Order Date]), 1)

MonthLabel = FORMAT(orders[MonthStart], "MMM YYYY")

Core Financial Measures

Total Revenue = SUM(orders[Revenue])

Total Profit = SUM(orders[Order Profit Per Order])

Units Sold = SUM(orders[Order Item Quantity])

Total Orders = DISTINCTCOUNT(orders[Order Id])

Operational Measures

Avg Profit = 
AVERAGE(orders[Order Profit Per Order])

Avg Shipping Days = 
AVERAGE(orders[Days for shipping (real)])

% of Total Orders = 
DIVIDE(
    [Total Orders],
    CALCULATE([Total Orders], ALL(orders[Order Status Lower]))
)

Dynamic Top-N Logic

Top = GENERATESERIES(5, 10, 5)
Selected Metric Value = 
SWITCH(
    TRUE(),
    SELECTEDVALUE('Metric Selector'[Metric Selector Fields]) = NAMEOF([Total Revenue]),  [Total Revenue],
    SELECTEDVALUE('Metric Selector'[Metric Selector Fields]) = NAMEOF([Total Profit]), [Total Profit]
)
Rank = 
SWITCH(
    TRUE(),

    SELECTEDVALUE('Geography Selector'[Geography Selector Fields]) = "'orders'[Market]",
    RANKX(
        ALLSELECTED('orders'[Market]),
        [Selected Metric Value],
        ,
        DESC,
        DENSE
    ),

    SELECTEDVALUE('Geography Selector'[Geography Selector Fields]) = "'orders'[Order Region]",
    RANKX(
        ALLSELECTED('orders'[Order Region]),
        [Selected Metric Value],
        ,
        DESC,
        DENSE
    ),

    SELECTEDVALUE('Geography Selector'[Geography Selector Fields]) = "'orders'[Order Country]",
    RANKX(
        ALLSELECTED('orders'[Order Country]),
        [Selected Metric Value],
        ,
        DESC,
        DENSE
    )
)
Dynamic Filter = 
VAR _selectednumber = SELECTEDVALUE('Top'[Top])
RETURN
IF(
    ISFILTERED('Top'),
    INT([Rank] <= _selectednumber),
    1
)
